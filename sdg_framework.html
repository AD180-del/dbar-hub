DBAR SDG Mandate: The 2030 Agenda

Alignment of Big Earth Data with Global Sustainability Goals

The 17 Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) represent the global community's most ambitious plan for a sustainable future. Adopted by all United Nations Member States in 2015, the SDGs are an urgent call for action to promote prosperity while protecting the planet.

"Through the pledge to Leave No One Behind, countries have committed to fast-track progress for those furthest behind first."

The Digital Belt and Road (DBAR) programme is specifically designed to bridge the data gap in the implementation of these goals, recognizing that social, economic, and environmental sustainability are deeply integrated.

The 17 Sustainable Development Goals

SDG 1

No Poverty

Ending poverty in all its forms by targeting vulnerability and supporting communities affected by conflict and climate disasters.

SDG 2

Zero Hunger

Ending hunger and malnutrition through sustainable agriculture and supporting small-scale food producers.

SDG 3

Good Health & Well-being

Ensuring healthy lives and promoting well-being at all ages, addressing inequalities and environmental threats.

SDG 4

Quality Education

Ensuring free, equitable primary and secondary schooling and vocational training for all.

SDG 5

Gender Equality

Achieving equality and empowering all women and girls by ending discrimination and violence.

SDG 6

Clean Water & Sanitation

Sustainable management of water resources and restoration of water-related ecosystems.

SDG 7

Affordable & Clean Energy

Ensuring universal access to renewable energy and improving global energy productivity.

SDG 8

Decent Work & Economic Growth

Promoting inclusive growth, technological innovation, and productive employment for all.

SDG 9

Industry, Innovation & Infrastructure

Building resilient infrastructure and fostering inclusive, sustainable industrialisation.

SDG 10

Reduced Inequalities

Promoting economic inclusion for all regardless of sex, race, or ethnicity through sound global policy.

SDG 11

Sustainable Cities & Communities

Making cities safe, resilient, and inclusive through improved urban planning and transport.

SDG 12

Responsible Consumption & Production

Changing production and consumption patterns to reduce the global ecological footprint.

SDG 13

Climate Action

Urgent action to combat climate change through adaptation and low-carbon development.

SDG 14

Life Below Water

Conserving oceans and marine resources and protecting coastal ecosystems from pollution.

SDG 15

Life on Land

Protecting terrestrial ecosystems, managing forests sustainably, and halting biodiversity loss.

SDG 16

Peace, Justice & Strong Institutions

Providing access to justice for all and building effective, accountable institutions at all levels.

SDG 17

Partnerships for the Goals

Strengthening global partnerships and cooperation to revitalise the 2030 Agenda.

The Executive backbone: CBAS

Announced by President Xi Jinping in 2020, the International Research Centre of Big Data for Sustainable Development Goals (CBAS) serves as the executive engine for the DBAR mandate.

SDG Data Infrastructure

Developing high-performance computing and AI-driven platforms for one-stop SDG indicator monitoring and evaluation.

SDG Satellite Constellation

Operating a dedicated series of SDG satellites to provide precise Earth Observation data for scientific research.

STI Think Tank

Providing Science, Technology, and Innovation (STI) solutions to support evidence-based policy making.

Capacity Building

Providing professional education and big data training to developing countries to enhance global capability.

CASEarth: The Technical Foundation

The research outcomes of the Big Earth Data Science Engineering Programme (CASEarth) provide the technical framework for the mandate. With over 1,200 researchers across 129 institutions, CASEarth facilitates the transition from data to actionable decision-making tools.
