DBAR Glossary

Definitions and descriptions for the DBAR Ecosystem

CBAS

International Research Centre of Big Data for Sustainable Development Goals.

Established in Beijing in 2021, CBAS serves as the permanent Secretariat and scientific headquarters for DBAR. It provides the technological backbone, including cloud computing and data repositories, for the entire international network.

ICoE (International Centre of Excellence)

Regional implementation nodes hosted by partner universities or research councils. ICoEs act as the physical interface between global DBAR data and local decision-making. Each ICoE typically leads a specific thematic area (e.g., ICoE-Helsinki leads Cold Regions research).

WG (Working Group)

Thematic research groups dedicated to specific domains (e.g., DBAR-AGRI, DBAR-HERITAGE). While ICoEs are physical locations, Working Groups are collaborative networks that define the research agendas and technical standards for their respective fields.

DBAR-DATA (Big Earth Data)

Chaired by: Prof. Li Guoqing

This group focuses on data standards, sharing protocols, and the interoperability of datasets across the Belt and Road. It oversees the CASEarth cloud infrastructure to ensure that data from different countries can be merged and analysed centrally.

DBAR-HiMAC (High Mountain and Cold Regions)

Chaired by: Prof. Qiu Yubao

It monitors glaciers, snow cover, and permafrost in the "Third Pole" (Himalayas) and the Arctic. This group is the scientific engine behind China‚Äôs Polar Silk Road ambitions.

DBAR-HERITAGE (Natural and Cultural Heritage)

Chaired by: Prof. Rosa Lasaponara

It uses satellite imagery to monitor and preserve UNESCO World Heritage sites at risk from climate change or human conflict. This is a primary entry point for collaboration with European archeological and cultural institutions.

DBAR-WATER (Water Resources)

Chaired by: Prof. Guo Huadong

Analyses water scarcity, river basin management, and drought. It is particularly active in Central and South Asia (e.g., the Indus and Mekong basins), providing the data used for "Water Diplomacy" and transboundary resource management.

DBAR-AGRI (Agriculture and Food Security)

Chaired by: Prof. Wu Bingfang

Focused on "Zero Hunger" (SDG 2). It uses Earth observation to estimate crop yields, monitor pest outbreaks, and manage land use. This group maps directly to centres in agricultural hubs like Thailand and Zambia.

DBAR-COAST (Coastal and Marine)

Chaired by: Prof. Guo Huadong

Monitors the "Maritime Silk Road." It tracks sea-level rise, coastal erosion, and mangrove health. It serves as the scientific link for port cities and island nations involved in the Blue Economy.

DBAR-URBAN (Sustainable Cities)

Chaired by: Prof. Martino Pesaresi

Tracks the rapid urbanisation of the Belt and Road. It uses "Nighttime Lights" data and urban heat map modelling to analyse how cities are expanding and where infrastructure is most needed.

DBAR-DISASTER (Disaster Risk Reduction)

Chaired by: Prof. Rajib Shaw

Focuses on "Space-to-Ground" disaster management. It provides rapid-response satellite mapping for earthquakes, floods, and landslides, positioning China as a primary provider of emergency data to developing nations.

DBAR-ENVI (Environmental Change)

Chaired by: Prof. Guo Huadong

It monitors carbon sinks, biodiversity loss, and ecosystem health. It bridges the gap between local environmental policy and global climate targets like the Paris Agreement.

SDGSAT-1

The Sustainable Development Goals Satellite-1, launched in November 2021. It is the first Earth observation satellite dedicated specifically to the UN 2030 Agenda. SDGSAT-1 provides high-resolution data for monitoring human-environment interactions.

CASEarth

The Big Earth Data Science Engineering Project. This is the cloud platform and infrastructure that supports DBAR. It facilitates the storage, processing, and public sharing of planetary-scale datasets.

Digital Belt and Road (DBAR)

A scientific programme initiated by the Chinese Academy of Sciences (CAS) to improve data sharing and scientific collaboration. Unlike the economic-focused DSR, DBAR is strictly epistemic, focusing on environmental monitoring and sustainable development milestones.
